summary: Extracting Audio from Videos 

If you want to extract audio from a video, you don't need external apps. You can use the 
*Apple Shortcuts* app and create an action to do it. I have searched this a dozen times, 
so here is how to do it:

1. **Open Shortcuts App**: Tap the `+` icon to create a new shortcut.
2. **Name It**: Tap the shortcut name (e.g., *"Shortcut 1"*) and rename it (for example *"Extract Audio"*).
3. **Share Sheet Settings**: Tap the 'i' icon at the bottom, enable "Show in Share Sheet," then tap "Done".
4. **Receive Media**: Tap the blue text under "Receive," clear all selections, and check only "Media," then tap "Done".
5. **Add "Encode Media"**: Search for and add the "Encode Media" action.
6. **Set to Audio Only**: Tap the arrow next to "Encode Media," expand the options, and ensure "Audio Only" is toggled on (M4A is the default format).
7. **Add "Save File"**: Search for and add the "Save File" action.
8. **Ask Where to Save**: Tap the arrow next to "Save File," and enable "Ask Where to Save".
